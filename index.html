00 summary/


[EXECUTIVE SUMMARY]

FAMILIARIZING THE FUTURE is an interactive art installation and social conversation designed to reframe how humans relate to robots and artificial intelligence. In an era where automation threatens to deepen economic inequality and corporate control, this project challenges that dystopian trajectory — discussing an alternative vision in which robotics become tools of collective liberation rather than exploitation.

[MISSION STATEMENT]

This exhibit aims to humanize robotics and democratize automation by using art as a bridge between people and machines. To transform fear of technology into curiosity, empowerment, and collective ownership. Our goal is a cultural and emotional shift where robots belong to all of us — not just the few who can afford to control them.

[CONCEPT OVERVIEW]

The exhibit merges sculpture, robotics, and wearable art into a seamless interactive environment. Visitors design, interact, and collaborate with robots as creative partners, dissolving the boundaries between art object and artificial intelligence. The result is a living dialogue about identity, automation, and what it means to be human in a mechanized world.

[SOCIAL IMPACT]

FAMILIARIZING THE FUTURE reframes robots as approachable, customizable, and emotional. It challenges the fear narratives driven by monopolized tech culture and proposes a world where automation can redistribute wealth and free humans from monotonous labor. The exhibit promotes empathy, imagination, and ownership — showing that when shared collectively, technology could serve humanity.

01 objectives/


[A DISCUSSION OF THE SERIOUS SOCIETAL IMPACT OF AUTOMATION]

By presenting robots as approachable, customizable, and emotional, this project dismantles fear narratives driven by tech monopolies and disruptor culture. It imagines a future where humans and machines collaborate — not compete — and where technological power benefits all.

[KEY THEMES]

• Automation as liberation, not displacement
• Technology as art and empathy
• Redistribution of resources through innovation
• Collective ownership of the automated world

02 scope/


[CONCEPT OVERVIEW]


The exhibit centers around the customization, interaction, and re-imagining of real robots — re-styled, tattooed, and adorned as living sculptures. Each robot becomes a symbol of identity, individuality, and co-evolution.
By merging the aesthetic language of street fashion, jewelry, tattoo culture, and human expression with robotic design, the project challenges the sterile, corporate look of current tech. It asks: What happens when humans reclaim the narrative of automation using visual and emotional connectors?

[CORE COMPONENTS]

• Create interactive experiences where visitors engage with robots in fun, creative ways, exploring the potential for robots to be both functional and stylish.
• Offer panel discussions on the societal implications of automation, focusing on how robotics can shape the future of work, leisure, and income distribution.
• Inspire a shift in how we view technology by making robots fashionable, relatable, and even cool.
• Encourage cross-industry collaboration between art, tech, and education communities to promote shared innovation.

04 the installation/

[THE OPENING — artist meet & greet + objectives]

meet and greet with digital questioneers with questions, suggestions, ideas about ethical automation, universal robotics access, and redistributing the benefits of automation across humanity/

[THE GALLERY nearest the ENTRANCE — "the fear box"]

A reflective space where attendees anonymously share their anxieties about the singularity and AI. These are displayed in real time to foster awareness, compassion, and collective discussion/

[THE GALLERY — interactive robot displays]

Visitors interact with a range of robots from RobotLab and other collaborators, including:
— A security robot with a distinct accent and personality - humorous, welcoming, and culturally mindful.
— A projection-mapping robot integrating digital art onto physical surfaces.
— A fashion-forward personal assistant robot that helps guests while remaining stylish and chic.
— A dog security robot adorned with gold teeth and jewelry - a playful twist on protection.
— STEM robots that serve as educational tools, allowing visitors to experiment with programming and control.

[THE GALLERY — customization stations]

Guests can personalize small robots with drawn tattoos, magnetic piercings, and add accessories, or program them with slang and dialogue to create unique identities — turning robotics into an act of self-expression/

[THE GALLERY — "ai as your mirror"]

A QR code with a question is posted on a projection wall; you ask AI on your phone and copy/paste the answer. These are displayed in real time to see how our digital personalities control the outcome of our individual online experience/

[THE GALLERY towards EXIT — “dream the machine”]

Visitors complete the phrase: “If I had a robot, I would…” Their responses feed into a projection sculpture that evolves in real time, revealing collective hopes and fears about automation/

[THE CLOSING Forum — “automation for ALL”]

live discussion where artists, technologists, and the public explore ethical automation, universal robotics access, and redistributing the benefits of automation across humanity/
